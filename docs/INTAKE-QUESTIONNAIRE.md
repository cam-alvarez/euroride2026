# Client Intake Questionnaire — Euroride 2026

The "agency-style" form the team would send the client (Dad). Answers refine
the product; unanswered ones have sensible defaults already shipped in v3
(noted in *italics*). Send back in any form — even voice notes.

## From the travel & moto lead

1. Is the EagleRider Milan booking confirmed? Exact pickup/drop-off address
   and times? (Drives the Day 1 / Day 11 briefs and the SOS rental card.)
2. Final headcount and bikes — 18 riders? Any passengers? Any riders who
   want shorter daily options flagged? *(Defaults to "18 riders" copy.)*
3. Which hotels are actually **booked** vs. suggestions? The app labels all
   lodging "Suggested · Not booked" until told otherwise.
4. Should Days 9–10 (flex days) stay open, or lock in the Como loops?
5. Group riding rules to publish? (Staggered formation, corner-man system,
   fuel-range of the thirstiest bike, comms channel.) Could become a
   "Riding Together" card under Essentials.
6. Does anyone need vignette purchase links pre-trip, or is that at pickup?

## From the UX designer

7. Who is the least tech-comfortable rider? Would they benefit from a
   printed one-page fallback? *(Print-friendly page is on the roadmap.)*
8. Spanish or English as the crew's default? *(App auto-detects the phone
   language; either can be set manually.)*
9. Any riders with accessibility needs — larger text, color-blindness?

## From the engineering side

10. Keep `github.io` URL or buy a domain (~$10/yr)?
11. For the chat assistant (Phase 4): OK creating a free Cloudflare account
    and an Anthropic/Gemini API key with a small budget cap (~$5)?
12. Should profiles eventually sync across devices (Phase 5, needs the free
    backend described in ACCOUNTS-AND-SYNC.md), or is per-device fine?

## From the PM

13. Hard deadline for "crew-ready" v1? (Suggest: 4 weeks before departure —
    2026-07-23 — so everyone installs it while still at home.)
14. Who besides Dad should be able to edit trip content?
15. Anything in the current app that should be cut? Fewer features done
    perfectly beats many done half-way.
